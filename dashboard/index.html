What is this?

This dashboard analyses the SPX 0DTE Expected Move (EM) — the daily price range the options market implies for the S&P 500 index on a given day, derived from the VIX (CBOE Volatility Index). Data is sourced from Polygon.io and covers 3+ years of 1-minute SPX bars plus VIX daily closes.

How the Expected Move is Calculated

When ATM 0DTE straddle data is available, the daily expected move is derived directly from the market-implied 1-day volatility (VIX1D):

σ = ATM_call_mid + ATM_put_mid   (straddle at open)

This captures the actual 1-day implied vol from 0DTE options prices — more accurate than the 30-day VIX for daily expected moves. When straddle data is unavailable, the fallback is:

σ = open_price × (VIX / 100) / √252

A 0.8σ band is also drawn as a tighter inner boundary.

The "Patches" — 4 Box Zones

During the first 5 minutes of RTH (9:31–9:35 ET), the indicator builds four coloured "patch" boxes by tracking the min and max of each EM level across those bars:

1σ Upper Box (green)
Range of (open + σ) values during lookback. Price closing here = bullish day that reached full EM upside.
0.8σ Upper Box (teal)
Range of (open + 0.8σ). Inner bullish zone. Close here = day pushed toward upper EM but didn't fully reach it.
0.8σ Lower Box (orange)
Range of (open − 0.8σ). Inner bearish zone. Close here = day pushed toward lower EM but held inner boundary.
1σ Lower Box (red)
Range of (open − σ). Price closing here = bearish day that reached full EM downside.

Additional Levels

PDC EM Lines (yellow dashed)
Previous Day Close ± daily EM. Shows whether today's open gapped inside or outside yesterday's expected range.
Buy-VW EM (lime)
Volume-weighted upper EM biased toward bullish candles during the 5-min lookback. Skewed VWAP upper anchor.
Sell-VW EM (red)
Volume-weighted lower EM biased toward bearish candles. Skewed VWAP lower anchor.
Skew Midpoint (grey)
Average of Buy-VW EM and Sell-VW EM. Acts as intraday equilibrium reference line.
Continuous VWAP
Session VWAP from 9:30 open, with ±1σ standard deviation bands computed from rolling volume-weighted variance.

Glossary — Features, Metrics & Definitions

Complete reference for every feature, metric, and term used across the dashboard. All features are computed at T = time_of_low from the lookback window [T−60 min, T] with no lookahead.

A — Price Momentum / Overextension vs Moving Average

FeatureComputation
stretch_{w} (w=5,10,20,60)(last_price − EMA_w) / (RV_std × last_price). Deviation from exponential moving average normalized by realized volatility.
ret_z_{w} (w=5,10,20,60)(cumulative_log_return − mean) / (std × √w). Standardized return z-score over w bars.
stretch_x_squeezestretch_20 × squeeze_encoded. Interaction: overextension during volatility compression.

B — Realized Variance, Jump Proxy, Semivariance

FeatureComputation
rv_{w} (w=5,20,60)Σ(log_return²) over w bars. Annualized: rv × 252 × 390. Measure of true intraday volatility.
bv_{w} (w=5,20,60)(√(2/π))⁻² × Σ(|ret_t| × |ret_{t−1}|). Bipower variance — robust to jumps, captures continuous vol only.
jump_share_{w} (w=5,20,60)max(RV − BV, 0) / RV. Fraction of variance attributable to jumps. 0 = continuous, 1 = all jumps.
down_sv_{w} (w=5,20,60)Σ(negative_returns²) / RV. Downside semivariance ratio. >0.5 = downside-biased volatility.
range_vol_{w} (w=5,20,60)mean(log(high/low)). Parkinson-style range volatility from intraday high-low bars.
rv_spikerv_5 / rv_60. Recent 5-bar RV relative to 60-bar baseline. >1 = volatility spike.
autocorr_{w} (w=10,20,30)Lag-1 autocorrelation of log returns. Negative = mean reversion tendency.

C — Volume / Order-Flow Proxies (SPY-derived)

FeatureComputation
rel_vol_{w} (w=5,10,20,30)mean(volume_w) / time_of_day_baseline_volume. Compares current volume to historical median for this minute-of-day.
signed_vol_imb_{w} (w=5,10,20,30)Σ(sign(Δprice) × volume) / Σ(volume). Range [−1, +1]: −1 = all sell, +1 = all buy.
cvd_slopeSlope of OLS on cumulative signed volume delta (30 bars), normalized by mean |CVD|. Positive = accumulation.
rv_spike_x_vol_imbrv_spike × signed_vol_imb_10. Interaction: volatility spike with directional volume imbalance.

D — Candle Patterns + Technical Oscillators

FeatureComputation
lower_wick_ratio(min(open, close) − low) / range. Bullish rejection signal: 0 = no wick, 1 = all wick.
upper_wick_ratio(high − max(open, close)) / range. Bearish rejection signal.
body_to_range|close − open| / range. Low = doji/indecision, high = strong conviction.
avg_body_range_5Mean body_to_range over last 5 bars.
avg_lower_wick_5Mean lower_wick_ratio over last 5 bars.
lower_wick_x_rv_spikelower_wick_ratio × rv_spike. Bullish rejection during vol spike = reversal setup.
rsi_14Standard 14-period RSI: 100 − 100/(1 + RS). <30 = oversold, >70 = overbought.
stoch_k_14(close − 14-bar low) / (14-bar high − 14-bar low) × 100. <20 = oversold.
bb_pct_b(close − lower_band) / (upper − lower). Bollinger %b. 0 = at lower band, 1 = at upper.
bb_bandwidth(upper − lower) / mid. Bollinger bandwidth. Low = squeeze, high = expansion.
bb_kc_squeeze1 if Bollinger Bands are inside Keltner Channels (BB upper < KC upper AND BB lower > KC lower). Squeeze signal.
macd_histEMA(12) − EMA(26) minus 9-period signal line. Positive = bullish momentum.
trend_slope_{w} (w=20,60)OLS slope of log(price) over w bars. Positive = uptrend.
trend_curvature_{w} (w=20,60)2nd-order coefficient of polynomial fit to log(price). Positive = accelerating up.

FeatureComputation
vix_intradayLatest 1-min VIX close (or fallback to previous day's VIX close).

vix_chg_5mVIX change over last 5 bars. Positive = fear rising.
vix_chg_30mVIX change over last 30 bars.

H — Options-Derived (Straddle Price + Implied-Realized Spread)

FeatureComputation
straddle_priceATM call + ATM put mid-price at open (from VIX1D straddle data), or SPX × VIX / (100 × √252) as fallback. 1σ expected move in points.

fall_normalizedfall_pts / vix_prev. Fall scaled by volatility regime.

is_afternoon1 if hour ≥ 13 ET.

PDC EMPrevious day close ± 1σ expected move. Yesterday's expected range projected forward.

Close ZoneWhere price closed: above_1sig, in_1sig_up_box, gap_upper, in_08sig_up_box, inside, in_08sig_dn_box, gap_lower, in_1sig_dn_box, below_1sig.
Gap ZoneGap classification: gap_up_beyond_em, gap_up_within_em, flat, gap_dn_within_em, gap_dn_beyond_em.
EM SourceSTR = straddle-derived (VIX1D), VIX = fallback VIX/√252 approximation.

Reversal Analysis — Key Metrics

MetricDefinition
Swing High / Swing LowHighest and lowest points within 30-min rolling window. Defines the fall event.
Fall Pointsswing_high − swing_low. Raw downside magnitude.
Fall Categorysmall (30–50 pts), medium (50–80 pts), large (80+ pts).
Reversal PointsMax high within 60 bars after swing low minus swing low. Recovery magnitude.

Squeeze DurationConsecutive bars with non-increasing rolling max of call body sizes (20-bar lookback).
Expansion Ratioexpansion_body / squeeze_min_body. Breakout strength after compression.

VIX1D Computation

MetricDefinition
Straddle PriceATM 0DTE call mid + put mid, averaged over first 5 RTH bars (9:31–9:35). Smooths opening noise.
VIX1D(straddle / SPX_open) × √252 × 100. Annualized 1-day implied volatility on VIX scale.
EM (1σ)Raw straddle price ≈ 0.798 × σ × SPX. Direct expected move in points.
ATM StrikeSPX price rounded to nearest 25 pts. Used for 0DTE option ticker construction.
Ticker FormatO:SPXW{YYMMDD}{C|P}{strike×1000:08d} — e.g. O:SPXW260406C05875000

Model Evaluation Metrics

MetricDefinition
PrecisionTP / (TP + FP). "If we predict reversal, does it actually reverse?" Primary optimization metric.
RecallTP / (TP + FN). "Of all true reversals, how many did we catch?" Constrained ≥ 20%.
F1 Score2 × (precision × recall) / (precision + recall). Harmonic mean balance metric.
Accuracy(TP + TN) / N. Overall correctness (less useful with imbalanced classes).
Brier ScoreMean squared error of predicted probabilities vs binary outcome. Lower = better calibrated.
Base RateΣ(y) / N. Prior probability of positive class (significant reversal).
Optimal ThresholdProbability cutoff tuned to maximize precision @ recall ≥ 20%. Typically 0.60–0.75.
