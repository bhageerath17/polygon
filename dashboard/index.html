What is this?

This dashboard analyses the SPX 0DTE Expected Move (EM) — the daily price range the options market implies for the S&P 500 index on a given day, derived from the VIX (CBOE Volatility Index). Data is sourced from Polygon.io and covers 3 months of 1-minute SPX bars (Jan–Apr 2026) plus VIX daily closes.

How the Expected Move is Calculated

The daily expected move (1σ) for any given bar is:

σ = open_price × (VIX / 100) / √252

This is the same formula used in the Pine Script indicator "SPX 0DTE EM (Skewed VWAP + Midpoint)". VIX is annualised volatility — dividing by √252 converts it to a single-day expected move. A 0.8σ band is also drawn as a tighter inner boundary.

The "Patches" — 4 Box Zones

During the first 5 minutes of RTH (9:31–9:35 ET), the indicator builds four coloured "patch" boxes by tracking the min and max of each EM level across those bars:

1σ Upper Box (green)
Range of (open + σ) values during lookback. Price closing here = bullish day that reached full EM upside.
0.8σ Upper Box (teal)
Range of (open + 0.8σ). Inner bullish zone. Close here = day pushed toward upper EM but didn't fully reach it.
0.8σ Lower Box (orange)
Range of (open − 0.8σ). Inner bearish zone. Close here = day pushed toward lower EM but held inner boundary.
1σ Lower Box (red)
Range of (open − σ). Price closing here = bearish day that reached full EM downside.

Additional Levels

PDC EM Lines (yellow dashed)
Previous Day Close ± daily EM. Shows whether today's open gapped inside or outside yesterday's expected range.
Buy-VW EM (lime)
Volume-weighted upper EM biased toward bullish candles during the 5-min lookback. Skewed VWAP upper anchor.
Sell-VW EM (red)
Volume-weighted lower EM biased toward bearish candles. Skewed VWAP lower anchor.
Skew Midpoint (grey)
Average of Buy-VW EM and Sell-VW EM. Acts as intraday equilibrium reference line.
Continuous VWAP
Session VWAP from 9:30 open, with ±1σ standard deviation bands computed from rolling volume-weighted variance.
